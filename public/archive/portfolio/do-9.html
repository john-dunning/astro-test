Digital Objectives
    
 

The full Scrabble board is visible when the user zooms out. Although the tiles are quite small, they are still completely functional. All the standard editing gestures continue to work, as does the handwriting recognition. The tiles will even dynamically expand to their full size when they are dragged outside of the board.

Double-tapping an already-played tile opens a glance widget displaying the tile’s full-size image and the name of the player who placed it. This is useful if more than two players are in the game. When a player receives the Newton on their turn, they won’t know which tiles the other two or three players have placed on the board.

The premium word and letter squares are abbreviated with small icons. Double-tapping an icon displays a glance widget indicating the square’s score value.

Unfortunately, when shown the Scrabble prototype Milton-Bradley deemed the Newton market too small to be worth pursuing. (The president and vice-president of Digital Objectives nevertheless continue to play cutthroat Scrabble games with it.)
