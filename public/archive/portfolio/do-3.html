Digital Objectives
    
 

The body outline interface handles multiple injuries cleanly and provides a natural mapping between the real world and the data entry task (even though I accidentally reversed the body’s left and right sides when first implementing the screen).

However, the first version has a few flaws, one of which is the amateurish illustration—in this version, it has been replaced with a professionally rendered one.

More problematic is that when multiple sites are selected, it is impossible to determine which one the injury list applies to without reading the label above the checkboxes. This forces users to switch their attention back and forth between visual and textual representations of the same information.

Also, the “Remove Injured Part” button, which deselects the currently selected part, has the unfortunate connotation of amputation.
